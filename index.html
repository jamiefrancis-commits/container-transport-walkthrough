Definition
What is Container
Transport?
The road haulage of standardised shipping containers between ports, depots, warehouses, and customer sites. It's the critical "last mile" and "first mile" that connects global ocean freight to the local supply chain.
Standardised Containers
ISO standard 20ft (TEU) and 40ft (FEU) sizes. Owned by shipping lines like Maersk, MSC, CMA CGM.
Container Hauliers
Specialist trucking companies with skeletal trailers, operating around port schedules and customs timelines.

Foundation
The 3-Address Order
Import and export are mirror images. Every container job has exactly 3 addresses.
📥 Import Order
Address 1Port (collect loaded)
Address 2Customer (deliver & unload)
Address 3Port/Depot (return empty)
📤 Export Order
Address 1Port/Depot (collect empty)
Address 2Customer (load goods)
Address 3Port (deliver loaded)
Setting the correct address types in Qargo determines the entire order flow

Haulier books VBS slot and collects with skeletal trailer

Delivery
Container driven to customer — live-unloaded or dropped on site

Empty Return

The entire import cycle typically takes 1–3 days from port collection to empty return
Operations
Key Workflows
Drop and Swap
Continuous rotation at high-volume sites — drop loaded, pick up empty in a loop. Maximises driver productivity.
Destuff & Distribute
Container unpacked at warehouse, goods redistributed across multiple outbound deliveries. Key freight forwarding workflow.
Leg Splits
Multi-day movements — deliver to customer, overnight at depot, return empty next day. Needs careful planning.
Intermodal
Containers travel by rail from southern ports to northern terminals before the final road leg. Reduces road miles and cost.
Challenges
The Cost of Getting It Wrong
Container transport has more moving parts than almost any other road freight operation.
⏰
Demurrage
Container sits at port beyond free days — £75–£150/day charges from the terminal.
📦
Detention
Container held off-terminal past the return deadline — charges from the shipping line.
🚢
Vessel Delays
Ship arrives late — all downstream haulage, warehouse bookings, and deliveries need replanning.
📋
Customs Holds
Container not cleared — driver arrives at port and can't collect. Wasted journey and VBS slot.
🔗
Multi-Party
Shipping lines, port terminals, customs, hauliers, and customers all need to be in sync.
Key Players
Who's Involved?
🚢 Shipping Lines
Own the containers and operate vessels. Maersk, MSC, CMA CGM, Hapag-Lloyd, COSCO, ONE, Evergreen.
🏗️ Port Terminals
Operate port infrastructure — unloading vessels, managing the yard, and collection/delivery via VBS.
🚛 Container Hauliers
Trucking companies that physically move containers on the road. From owner-drivers to large fleets.
📋 Freight Forwarders
Arrange the end-to-end journey — booking ocean freight, customs, and road haulage. Often subcontract the road leg.
🛃 Customs Brokers
Handle import/export declarations. UK uses CHIEF and CDS systems; Ireland uses AIS/AEP.
📦 Shippers / Consignees
The businesses sending or receiving the goods inside the container.

The Market
Major UK Container Ports
Felixstowe
UK's busiest — ~40% of all UK container traffic. Landguard & Trinity terminals. Operated by Hutchison Ports.
London Gateway
DP World's deep-water port in Thurrock, Essex. Growing rapidly. Strong for Far East and Middle East trade.
Southampton
Major south coast port by DP World. Key for trade with Asia, Mediterranean, and the Americas.
Tilbury
On the Thames in Essex. Containers, RORO, and bulk cargo. Significant for short-sea European trade.
Liverpool
Peel Ports' Liverpool2 terminal — serves North of England and Midlands, reducing long road hauls from the South East.
Regional
Bristol, Teesport, Grangemouth — serving specific trade lanes and local industry in SW, NE, and Scotland.
Many Qargo customers — TES, Stateline, On-Time Logistics — operate daily out of these ports.

Reference
Key Terms You'll Hear
TEU / FEU
Twenty-foot / Forty-foot Equivalent Unit — the standard container size measures.
Demurrage
Port terminal charges when a container stays in the yard beyond free storage.
Detention
Shipping line charges when a container is held off-terminal beyond free time.
Destin8
UK platform showing customs clearance and container release status at all major ports.
VBS
Vehicle Booking System — the port's slot booking system for collections and deliveries.
ETSF
Empty Container Storage Facility — off-port depots like Pentalver for empty returns.
Skeletal Trailer
Lightweight chassis trailer designed specifically to carry shipping containers.
Destuff
Unloading a container at warehouse — also called "stripping" or "devanning".
Drop and Swap
Leave loaded container, pick up empty in one trip — maximises driver utilisation.
Reefer
Temperature-controlled (refrigerated) container for perishable goods.
