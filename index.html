Definition
What is Container
Transport?
The road haulage of standardised shipping containers between ports, depots, warehouses, and customer sites. It's the critical "last mile" and "first mile" that connects global ocean freight to the local supply chain.
Standardised Containers
ISO standard 20ft (TEU) and 40ft (FEU) sizes. Owned by shipping lines like Maersk, MSC, CMA CGM.
Container Hauliers
Specialist trucking companies with skeletal trailers, operating around port schedules and customs timelines.

Operations
Key Workflows
Drop and Swap
Continuous rotation at high-volume sites ГўВЂВ” drop loaded, pick up empty in a loop. Maximises driver productivity.
Destuff & Distribute
Container unpacked at warehouse, goods redistributed across multiple outbound deliveries. Key freight forwarding workflow.
Leg Splits
Multi-day movements ГўВЂВ” deliver to customer, overnight at depot, return empty next day. Needs careful planning.
Intermodal
Containers travel by rail from southern ports to northern terminals before the final road leg. Reduces road miles and cost.
